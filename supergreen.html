SuperGreen: Opus Signium Innovatum

IDE SuperGreen Module / 2025

Material Science Physical Making Sustainability

This project is a modern interpretation of Roman concrete. The material, known as cocciopesto, is a lime-based mix that uses crushed brick and metakaolin clay as pozzolanic agents. The outcome is a stool that draws from classical Roman forms while landing in a contemporary brutalist language. It was designed primarily for outdoor use, where exposure to rain activates the material's self-healing properties over time.

The form takes its cues from ancient Roman furniture, particularly the curule chair. It was built through a layered fabrication process: a structural base of large brick fragments topped with a fiberglass-reinforced layer. The finished piece weighs roughly 75kg.

The pink tint in the surface comes from crushed brick dust mixed into the concrete. Surface textures were left to emerge naturally from the casting process rather than being forced into precision — an intentional decision to let the material express itself.
