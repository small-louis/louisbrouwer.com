LeveL: Gambling Harm Prevention

IDE Group Project / 2025

Service Design Scientific Method Software (Swift) Hardware

The Problem

Gambling harm affects millions of people, yet most interventions arrive too late — after significant financial and emotional damage has already occurred. Existing tools tend to focus on self-exclusion or spending limits, which require the user to already recognise they have a problem.

The System

LeveL is a multi-layered harm prevention system that combines vibrotactile stimulation, peer support, open banking integration, and geo-activation. Rather than waiting for crisis, the system intervenes in real time — at the point of gambling — using physiological and contextual signals.

The core hardware is a wearable device that delivers rhythmic vibrotactile stimulation to stabilise mood and sharpen cognition during gambling sessions. This is paired with a Swift application that connects to open banking APIs for spending awareness and uses geo-fencing to detect when a user enters a gambling venue.

Validation

We designed and executed a double-blind controlled trial to measure the effect of vibrotactile stimulation on gambling decision-making. I built the measurement hardware and the Swift application used in the trial. The study provided statistically significant evidence for the intervention's effectiveness.
