Imperial College Rocketry: Pluto

ICLR Structures Sub-team / 2024 – 2025

Aerospace Structural Analysis FEA Leadership

The Rocket

Pluto is a liquid bi-propellant sounding rocket designed and built by Imperial College London Rocketry, a student team of roughly 150 members. It stands 4.5 metres tall, weighs 81 kg at lift-off, and is powered by GOOFY, a 6 kN student-researched-and-developed regeneratively cooled engine running on nitrous oxide and isopropanol. The vehicle was entered into the 9 km apogee category at EuRoC 2025, the European Rocketry Challenge held in Portugal.

Stringer Design

I led the design of Pluto's stringer system within the structures sub-team, supervising two undergraduate engineers through the process. The airframe uses a skeletal architecture rather than a conventional skin — three aluminium 6082-T6 stringers arranged in threefold symmetry, aligned with the fin positions, form the primary load path between the engine and the propellant tank.

The stringers carry the full 6 kN compressive thrust load from the engine truss up into the tank's lower bulkhead. Initial sizing was done through Euler buckling analysis, with the critical load calculated as Pcr = π²EI / Leff². The profiles were then mass-optimised by introducing cut-out holes along their length, reducing weight without compromising structural integrity.

Simulation & Validation

Detailed buckling simulations were carried out in Abaqus CAE to validate the final geometry. The first buckling mode returned a safety factor of 3.6, confirming adequate margin against the design load. The analysis captured both local and global buckling behaviour to ensure the cut-out pattern did not introduce unexpected failure modes.

The stringers were water-jet cut from aluminium plate and connect at their base to a generatively designed engine truss. That truss, additively manufactured in AlSi10Mg, was compression tested to the full 6 kN engine load before being cleared for flight.

Vehicle Overview

  • Predicted apogee: 4,608 m AGL
  • Maximum speed: 296 m/s (Mach 0.88)
  • Peak acceleration: 5.64 g
  • Burn time: 9.1 seconds
  • Total flight time: 238.8 seconds
  • Engine: 6 kN SRAD regeneratively cooled (pintle injector, 7+ successful hot-fires)
  • Propellant tank: common bulkhead, 18.5 kg
  • Recovery: reefed parachute with non-pyrotechnic clamp-band separation
